Matthew Perry

About

Matthew Langford Perry was an American and Canadian actor. Best known for his role as Chandler Bing on the NBC television sitcom Friends, Perry also appeared on Ally McBeal and received Primetime Emmy Award nominations for his performances in The West Wing and The Ron Clark Story.

Perry in 2013

  • August 19, 1969: Matthew Langford Perry is born in Williamstown, Massachusetts, USA.
  • 1987: Perry moves to Los Angeles to pursue an acting career after finishing high school.
  • 1987-1996: He appears in various TV shows, including guest roles on "Charles in Charge," "Silver Spoons," and "Growing Pains."
  • 1994-2004: Matthew Perry gains widespread fame for his role as Chandler Bing on the hugely successful television sitcom "Friends," which runs for ten seasons.
  • 1997: Perry makes his feature film debut in "Fools Rush In," starring opposite Salma Hayek.
  • 2006-2007: He stars in the television series "Studio 60 on the Sunset Strip," which, despite critical acclaim, is short-lived.
  • 2011-2012: Perry stars in the sitcom "Mr. Sunshine," which he co-created and produced, but it is canceled after one season.
  • 2015: Perry stars in the CBS reboot of "The Odd Couple," playing the role of Oscar Madison.
  • 2016: He makes his playwriting and stage debut with the play "The End of Longing," which he also stars in.
  • 2021: Perry joins his former "Friends" cast members for a highly anticipated reunion special, which is released on HBO Max.
